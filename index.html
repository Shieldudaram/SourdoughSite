Sourdough

Learn the art of baking sourdough bread, from the starter to the final loaf.

Sourdough Boule

Ingredients

  • 10.93oz/310 g of water
  • 7.05oz/200 g of starter
  • 2 teaspoons/6g/.21oz of salt
  • 14.11oz/400 g all-purpose flour
  • 1.76oz/50 g wheat flour

Book 1: The Starter

Chapter 1: Creation

We have a starter so I’m skipping this for now.

Chapter 2: Maintenance

Ideally the sourdough starter should be fed daily. At the same time each day. It should help the growing yeast as it is a living creature. For maintaining it, feed it a 1:1:1 ratio of starter: flour: water, use King Arthur’s whole wheat flour and filtered water for the best results.

Chapter 3: The Feed Before

Before baking, feed the starter a 1:5:5 or 1:10:10 ratio of starter: flour: water. This will give the yeast more material to consume, making it more active before baking. Note that it will likely also take more time to rise enough to be ready for use.

Chapter 4: Finalé

Once the starter is just over double in size, it should be ready for use. It should be relatively airy and have a dome-shaped top. If it’s sunken in the middle, it may have been too long. A good test is a float test: if a spoonful of sourdough floats in a cup of warm water, it's ready to use.

Chapter 5: Tips and Tricks

The room temperature will affect how quickly the starter rises; the warmer the room, the quicker it will rise.

Chapter 6: Storage

Ours just stays on the counter, but I could write a spot about it going in the fridge.

Chapter 7: Discard

Mix 10.93oz/310 g of water, 7.05oz/200 g of starter, 2 teaspoons/6g/.21oz of salt, 14.11oz/400 g all-purpose flour, and 1.76oz/50 g wheat flour.

Mix with a spoon until shaggy, then use hands. Let sit in a warm place for 30 minutes, then perform stretch and folds a few times over the next few hours (4 times over two hours then rest one hour).

Flour dough and place it in a round banneton, shape it, and let it rest covered for an hour and a half. Preheat the oven to 500°F with a Dutch oven inside. Transfer the dough to the Dutch oven lined with parchment paper and semolina flour, set the temp to 485°F, and cook for 18/25 minutes. Remove the lid and cook for an additional 25/18 minutes at 465°F or 450°F for 30 minutes covered and then 15 uncovered.
